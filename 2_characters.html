Meet the Gang

Story Characters

ARTHUR MORGAN

"We're thieves in a world that don't want us no more."

A man haunted by loyalty and the weight of his past. Morgan stands as the most complex soul in the Van Der Linde brotherhood.

DUTCH VAN DER LINDE

"We need more money and time."

The charismatic outlaw leader with grand visions and dangerous plans. A man torn between his ideals and the brutal reality of the changing West.

SADIE ADLER

"I'm gonna kill every last one of them."

A widow turned warrior. Sadie transforms from a grieving rancher's wife into one of the gang's most fearless and deadly members, driven by vengeance and survival.

CHARLES SMITH

"We get shot or we shoot them."

A skilled hunter and tracker of mixed heritage. Charles brings quiet strength and moral clarity to the gang, always ready to fight for what's right.

JOHN MARSTON

"We all got to go sometime."

Dutch's protégé struggling to find his place. A loyal outlaw torn between his duties to the gang and his responsibilities to his family.

JAVIER ESCUELLA

"I am a cynic. I see bad in everything."

A Mexican revolutionary and skilled gunslinger. Javier's fierce loyalty to Dutch conflicts with his growing doubts about the gang's direction.

LENNY SUMMERS

"This is America, where a lying, cheating degenerate like myself can prosper."

A young, educated outlaw with a quick wit and sharp mind. Lenny brings intelligence and optimism to the gang despite the harsh realities around him.

SEAN MACGUIRE

"I love being an outlaw!"

An Irish hothead full of bravado and humor. Sean's loud personality and reckless courage make him both a liability and a beloved member of the gang.

MICAH BELL

"I'm a survivor, Black Lung."

A manipulative and ruthless outlaw. Micah's self-serving nature and cruel methods create tension within the gang, earning him distrust from most members.

BILL WILLIAMSON

"I ain't got no imagination."

A hot-tempered and insecure enforcer. Bill's loyalty to Dutch is unquestioning, but his volatile nature and need for acceptance make him dangerous.

HOSEA MATTHEWS

"It's a fine night for it."

Dutch's oldest friend and the gang's wise counselor. Hosea's intelligence and pragmatism balance Dutch's idealism, serving as the gang's voice of reason.

ABIGAIL ROBERTS

"We can't change what's done, we can only move on."

A former working girl turned devoted mother. Abigail fights fiercely for her son Jack and struggles to keep John on the right path.

JACK MARSTON

"I want to be just like you and papa when I grow up."

John and Abigail's young son, innocent yet curious about the outlaw life. Jack represents the hope for a better future beyond the gang.

MARY-BETH GASKILL

"I may be a thief, but I ain't no liar."

A sweet-natured romantic with nimble fingers. Mary-Beth dreams of becoming a writer while using her skills as a pickpocket for the gang.

KAREN JONES

"I rob banks, not old ladies."

A tough and witty con artist with a drinking problem. Karen's bravado masks deep insecurities as she participates in the gang's more dangerous schemes.

UNCLE

"I got lumbago. It's very serious."

The camp's lazy but lovable drunk. Uncle claims various ailments to avoid work, but his stories and humor keep spirits up around the campfire.

REVEREND SWANSON

"We can't always fight nature, John."

A fallen preacher battling addiction. Swanson seeks redemption while struggling with his demons, offering spiritual guidance despite his own failings.

JOSIAH TRELAWNY

"I am a conjurer, not a con man."

A flamboyant trickster and part-time member. Trelawny's mysterious double life and elaborate schemes make him both useful and unpredictable.

TILLY JACKSON

"I ain't going back to that life."

A young woman who escaped a worse gang. Tilly seeks a normal life and brings kindness and practicality to the camp's women.

SUSAN GRIMSHAW

"This camp ought to be ashamed of itself."

The camp's stern matriarch and enforcer. Miss Grimshaw runs a tight ship, keeping everyone in line with her sharp tongue and unwavering standards.

SIMON PEARSON

"I was in the Navy, you know."

The camp's portly cook and merchant. Pearson feeds the gang while boring them with tales of his supposed naval adventures.

KIERAN DUFFY

"I'm one of you now, ain't I?"

A former O'Driscoll Boy turned hostage. Kieran desperately tries to prove his loyalty to the Van der Linde gang despite constant suspicion.

LEOPOLD STRAUSS

"Everyone pays their debts, eventually."

The gang's cold-hearted loan shark. Strauss profits from desperate people's misery, handling the gang's most morally questionable financial dealings.
